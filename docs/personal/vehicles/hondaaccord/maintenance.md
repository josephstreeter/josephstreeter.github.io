## 2016 Honda Accord Sport Maintenance Guide

## Overview

This page is a maintenance reference for the 2016 Honda Accord Sport equipped with the 2.4L DOHC i-VTEC K24W2 engine. It covers fluid capacities, service intervals, required tools, and step-by-step procedures for all routine maintenance items.

## Honda Maintenance Minder

The 2016 Accord uses Honda's Maintenance Minder system to calculate service intervals based on driving conditions rather than fixed mileage. The system displays a percentage of remaining oil life and sub-item codes when additional service is due.

| Code | Service Required |
| --- | --- |
| A | Engine oil change |
| B | Engine oil and filter, tire rotation, brake inspection, steering/suspension inspection |
| 1 | Tire rotation |
| 2 | Air filter, cabin air filter, drive belt inspection |
| 3 | Transmission/CVT fluid |
| 4 | Spark plugs, timing belt/chain inspection, valve clearance |
| 5 | Engine coolant |
| 6 | Rear differential fluid (not applicable — FWD) |

## Scheduled Maintenance Intervals

### Engine Oil and Filter

- **Interval:** Honda Maintenance Minder (typically every 7,500-10,000 miles with 0W-20 synthetic)
- **Oil:** 0W-20 full synthetic meeting Honda specification
- **Filter:** Honda 15400-PLM-A01 or equivalent
- **Capacity:** 4.4 quarts with filter change

**Required Tools:**

- 17mm socket (drain plug)
- Oil filter wrench (64mm, 14-flute cap style for Honda)
- Drain pan
- Torque wrench
- Funnel
- Floor jack and jack stands or drive-up ramps
- Nitrile gloves
- New crush washer (Honda recommends replacing at each oil change)

**Steps:**

1. Warm the engine to operating temperature, then shut it off
2. Raise the front of the vehicle on ramps or jack stands. The drain plug is on the driver-side rear of the oil pan
3. Place a drain pan under the oil pan. Remove the 17mm drain plug and allow the oil to drain completely (10-15 minutes)
4. Inspect the crush washer. Replace it with a new Honda drain plug washer — reusing a deformed washer risks seepage
5. Reinstall the drain plug with a new crush washer and torque to **29 ft-lbs**
6. Locate the oil filter on the front of the engine block near the bottom. Place the drain pan underneath, then remove the oil filter with the filter wrench
7. Wipe the filter mounting flange clean with a rag. Apply a thin film of fresh oil to the new filter gasket
8. Thread the new filter on by hand until the gasket seats, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn
9. Add 4.4 quarts of 0W-20 synthetic through the valve cover fill cap using a funnel
10. Start the engine and watch for the oil pressure warning light to extinguish within 5 seconds. Inspect the drain plug and filter for leaks
11. Shut the engine off, wait 5 minutes, and verify the oil level on the dipstick
12. Reset the Maintenance Minder: ignition ON (engine off), use the Select/Reset stem on the instrument cluster to navigate to the oil life display, then hold the reset button for approximately 10 seconds until the indicator resets to 100%

### Air Filter (Engine)

- **Interval:** Every 30,000 miles, or when Maintenance Minder code 2 appears
- **Part:** Honda 17220-5A2-A00 or equivalent

**Required Tools:**

- No tools required (tool-free clips)
- Compressed air or shop vacuum (optional, for cleaning housing)

**Steps:**

1. Locate the air filter housing on the passenger side of the engine compartment
2. Release the 4 spring clips securing the housing cover
3. Lift the cover and remove the old air filter element
4. Inspect the inside of the housing for dirt, leaves, or debris and vacuum it clean
5. Hold the old filter up to light — if light does not pass through the filter media, replace it
6. Insert the new filter with the rubber seal seated firmly in the housing groove
7. Reinstall the cover and secure all 4 clips

### Spark Plugs

- **Interval:** Every 30,000 miles, or when Maintenance Minder code 4 appears
- **Type:** NGK Iridium ILZKR7B-11S (OEM specification)
- **Gap:** 0.039-0.043 in (factory pre-gapped — do not adjust iridium plugs)
- **Quantity:** 4

**Required Tools:**

- 5/8 in spark plug socket with rubber insert
- 3/8 in ratchet and 3-6 in extensions
- Torque wrench
- 10mm socket (ignition coil bolts)
- Dielectric grease (for coil boots)
- Spark plug boot puller (optional but recommended)
- Compressed air (to clear debris from plug wells before removal)

**Steps:**

1. Allow the engine to cool completely — at least 1 hour after last operation
2. Remove the plastic engine cover by pulling straight up (quarter-turn fasteners on some trims)
3. Label coil locations (1-4 from timing chain end) before disconnecting anything
4. Disconnect the electrical connector on ignition coil #1. Press the tab and pull straight up
5. Remove the single 10mm bolt securing the ignition coil and pull the coil straight up and out of the plug well
6. Direct a short burst of compressed air into the plug well to remove any debris before opening the combustion chamber
7. Insert the spark plug socket and extension into the well and remove the old spark plug
8. Inspect the old plug: black sooty deposit = rich mixture or oil fouling; white/grey = normal; blistered or melted electrode = overheating
9. Thread the new plug in by hand carefully to avoid cross-threading (aluminum head)
10. Torque to **13 ft-lbs** — do not over-torque
11. Apply a small amount of dielectric grease to the inside of the coil boot
12. Reinstall the ignition coil, push it firmly down until it clicks onto the plug, install the 10mm bolt, and reconnect the electrical connector
13. Repeat for all 4 cylinders
14. Reinstall the engine cover

### CVT Fluid

- **Interval:** Every 90,000 miles under normal conditions; every 30,000 miles for severe duty (frequent stop-and-go, city driving, or light towing)
- **Fluid:** Honda HCF-2 exclusively — no aftermarket substitutes are approved. Incorrect fluid will damage the CVT

**Required Tools:**

- 3/8 in square drive ratchet (drain and fill plugs)
- Drain pan
- Fluid pump or squeeze bottle
- 3.1 quarts Honda HCF-2 CVT fluid
- Shop rags
- Nitrile gloves

**Steps:**

1. Drive the vehicle for 10-15 minutes to warm the CVT fluid for more complete drainage
2. Raise the front of the vehicle and support on jack stands
3. Locate the CVT oil pan beneath the transmission — the drain plug is at the lowest point
4. Locate the fill plug on the side of the CVT case. Remove the **fill plug first** to confirm it is not seized before draining
5. Place a drain pan under the CVT and remove the drain plug. Allow to drain completely (10 minutes)
6. Reinstall the drain plug and torque to **36 ft-lbs**
7. Pump fresh Honda HCF-2 fluid into the fill plug hole until fluid begins to seep out (approximately 3.1 quarts)
8. Reinstall the fill plug and torque to **36 ft-lbs**
9. Lower the vehicle and start the engine. Shift through all positions (P-R-N-D-S) and return to Park
10. Check the fluid level using the dipstick (if equipped) or by briefly cracking the fill plug while at operating temperature (170-180°F) to confirm the level is at or near the fill hole

### Manual Transmission Fluid (6-Speed Models)

- **Interval:** Every 60,000 miles
- **Fluid:** Honda Manual Transmission Fluid (MTF) or equivalent Honda-approved MTF

**Required Tools:**

- 3/8 in square drive ratchet
- Fluid pump or squeeze bottle
- 2.2 quarts Honda MTF
- Drain pan

**Steps:**

1. Raise the vehicle and support on jack stands
2. Locate the transmission fill plug (side of the transmission case) and drain plug (bottom)
3. Remove the fill plug first, then the drain plug, and allow the fluid to drain
4. Reinstall the drain plug and tighten securely
5. Fill with Honda MTF through the fill hole until fluid seeps from the opening (approximately 2.2 quarts)
6. Reinstall the fill plug

### Coolant

- **Interval:** First change at 10 years or 120,000 miles; then every 5 years or 60,000 miles
- **Type:** Honda Type 2 OAT coolant (blue-green, pre-mixed 50/50)

> [!NOTE]
> Honda Type 2 OAT is not compatible with Dex-Cool, universal green, or other OAT formulations. Do not mix coolant types.

**Required Tools:**

- Pliers (lower radiator hose clamp)
- Large drain pan (minimum 2-gallon capacity)
- Funnel
- Distilled water (for flush)
- 6.3 quarts Honda Type 2 OAT pre-mixed coolant
- Coolant test strips or refractometer (optional)

**Steps:**

1. Allow the engine to cool completely. Do not open the cooling system when hot
2. Place a drain pan under the radiator. The drain petcock is on the lower passenger-side corner of the radiator
3. Remove the radiator cap to allow air in, then open the petcock and drain the coolant
4. Close the petcock. Fill the system with distilled water through the radiator neck
5. Start the engine with the heater set to maximum heat and run to operating temperature. Shut off and allow to cool completely
6. Drain the distilled water rinse through the petcock. Repeat the flush if the water is still discolored
7. Close the petcock. Fill slowly with Honda Type 2 OAT pre-mixed coolant
8. Leave the radiator cap off and start the engine. Set the heater to maximum heat. Watch the radiator neck — coolant level will drop as air pockets purge. Top off as needed
9. Once the thermostat opens (upper radiator hose becomes hot to the touch) and bubbles stop, install the radiator cap
10. Check the coolant overflow/surge tank level and top off to the FULL cold mark
11. Allow the engine to cool and recheck the level. Top off as needed

### Brake Fluid

- **Interval:** Every 3 years, regardless of mileage
- **Type:** DOT 3

**Required Tools:**

- Turkey baster or brake fluid vacuum pump (for reservoir extraction)
- Clear tubing and collection bottle (for bleeding)
- 8mm or 10mm box-end wrench (bleeder screws)
- Fresh DOT 3 brake fluid
- Nitrile gloves (brake fluid is corrosive to paint)

**Steps:**

1. Locate the brake fluid reservoir on the master cylinder (driver-side firewall)
2. Use a turkey baster or vacuum pump to remove as much old fluid from the reservoir as possible
3. Fill the reservoir with fresh DOT 3 fluid
4. Begin bleeding at the wheel farthest from the master cylinder — right rear
5. Attach clear tubing to the bleeder screw on the right rear caliper and run the other end into a collection bottle with a small amount of fluid covering the tube end (to prevent air re-entry)
6. Open the bleeder screw 1/4 turn. Have an assistant press the brake pedal slowly and hold it
7. Close the bleeder screw before releasing the pedal. Release the pedal. Repeat until fluid coming out is clear and bubble-free
8. Keep the reservoir topped off during the entire procedure — do not allow it to run dry
9. Repeat at left rear, right front, left front
10. Fill the reservoir to the MAX line and inspect all bleeders for leaks

### Cabin Air Filter

- **Interval:** Every 15,000-30,000 miles, or when Maintenance Minder code 2 appears
- **Part:** Honda 80292-T2F-A01 or equivalent

**Required Tools:**

- No tools required

**Steps:**

1. Open the glove box and remove all contents
2. Press inward on both sides of the glove box door to release the hinge stoppers, allowing the door to drop down past its normal stop
3. Look behind the glove box opening for the cabin filter housing — it will have a tab or cover at the front
4. Open the housing cover and slide the old filter straight out
5. Note the airflow direction arrow printed on the filter frame
6. Insert the new filter with the arrow pointing in the same direction (typically downward toward the blower motor)
7. Close the housing cover and lift the glove box door back into its normal stops

### Tires

- **Front pressure:** 33 PSI cold
- **Rear pressure:** 33 PSI cold
- **Rotation interval:** Every 7,500 miles
- **Lug nut torque:** 80 ft-lbs

> [!NOTE]
> The 19-inch 235/40R19 tires have a 40-series sidewall. Pressure loss is not always visually obvious — check with a gauge frequently.

**Required Tools:**

- Accurate tire pressure gauge (digital recommended)
- Floor jack and jack stands (or lift)
- 21mm socket (lug nuts)
- Torque wrench

**Pressure Check Steps:**

1. Check pressures cold (before driving more than 1 mile)
2. Remove the valve stem cap and press the gauge firmly onto the valve stem
3. Add or release air to reach 33 PSI front and rear
4. Reinstall valve caps

**Rotation Steps:**

1. Loosen all lug nuts on wheels to be rotated before raising the vehicle
2. Raise one end of the vehicle and support on jack stands
3. Perform a front-to-rear same-side rotation (straight rotation — no cross-rotation for FWD vehicles unless tires are non-directional):
   - Left front → Left rear
   - Left rear → Left front
   - Right front → Right rear
   - Right rear → Right front
4. Torque all lug nuts to **80 ft-lbs** in a star pattern
5. Re-torque after 50 miles

### Drive Belt Inspection

- **Interval:** Inspect every 30,000 miles; replace at 105,000 miles or at first sign of wear
- **Notes:** The 2016 K24W2 uses a **timing chain** (no timing belt service required). The drive belt referred to in Maintenance Minder code 2 is the **serpentine/accessory drive belt**

**Required Tools:**

- Inspection flashlight
- Straight-edge or ruler (belt deflection check)

**Steps:**

1. With the engine off and cool, inspect the serpentine belt on the front of the engine
2. Look for cracks across the ribs, fraying on the edges, missing chunks, or glazing on the rib surfaces
3. Check belt tension by pressing on the belt midway between pulleys — there should be minimal deflection (less than 1/2 in under moderate force)
4. Inspect each idler and tensioner pulley by spinning them by hand. They should spin freely with no grinding or rumbling
5. If any wear is found or the belt has reached 105,000 miles, replace it

### Wiper Blades

- **Interval:** Replace annually or when streaking, chattering, tearing, or reduced visibility occurs
- **Type:** Bosch ICON (beam-style)
- **Size:** Driver 26 in (26A), Passenger 22 in (22A) — verify fitment for your specific trim before purchase
- **Rear:** Not applicable for the 2016 Accord sedan (no rear wiper)

**Required Tools:**

- No tools required
- Small flat-blade screwdriver (optional)

**Steps:**

1. Turn the wipers off and set the ignition to ACCESSORY or OFF. Lift the wiper arms away from the windshield and rest them in the service position if available. Place a towel on the windshield to protect the glass.
2. Rotate the blade assembly perpendicular to the arm so the release tab or clip is accessible.
3. Press the blade release tab (or lift the small locking clip) and slide the old blade off the arm adapter.
4. Compare the new Bosch ICON blade adapter with the old blade to confirm compatibility.
5. Align the new blade with the arm adapter and slide it on until it locks with an audible click.
6. Carefully lower the wiper arm back onto the windshield.
7. Test the wipers at low speed and verify there is no streaking, skipping, or interference with the cowl or hood.

**Installation Tips:**

- Keep a towel or rag between the arm and windshield while changing blades.
- Replace both blades at the same time for consistent performance.
- If the blade chatters or streaks after installation, verify the arm is not bent and that the blade is the correct length and properly seated.
- Always verify blade length and adapter type against the parts listing or Bosch fitment guide before purchasing.
