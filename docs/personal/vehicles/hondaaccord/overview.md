## 2016 Honda Accord Sport

## Overview

The 2016 Honda Accord Sport is a mid-size sedan in Honda's long-running Accord lineup. The Sport trim occupies the middle of the Accord range and adds a sportier aesthetic over the base LX — including a 19-inch wheel upgrade, a rear spoiler, a sport-tuned suspension, and a more aggressive front fascia — while retaining the refined, practical character the Accord is known for. It is powered by a naturally aspirated 2.4L four-cylinder engine and is available with either a 6-speed manual or a continuously variable transmission (CVT).

## Sport Trim Highlights

The Sport trim includes the following features not found on the base LX:

- 19-inch aluminum alloy wheels (unique to Sport)
- Sport-tuned suspension (stiffer springs and damping)
- Rear lip spoiler
- Body-color side sills
- LED daytime running lights
- Honda Sensing safety suite (on CVT-equipped models from mid-2016 production)
- 8-inch Honda Display Audio touchscreen with Android Auto and Apple CarPlay
- Heated front seats
- Dual-zone automatic climate control
- 192-watt, 8-speaker audio system
- Remote keyless entry with push-button start

## Known Issues and Common Concerns

| Issue | Description | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CVT hesitation | Slight hesitation on light throttle acceleration, particularly from a stop | Honda issued software updates for the CVT ECU — check for open TSBs |
| 19-inch tire ride quality | The 235/40R19 tires have a short sidewall, resulting in a firmer ride over rough pavement | Consider 17-inch wheel swap for smoother ride if desired |
| Oil consumption | Some 2016 K24W2 engines consume a small amount of oil between changes | Check level at each fuel fill or at least monthly |
| Fuel door spring | Fuel door spring can weaken over time, causing the door to not spring open fully | Inexpensive repair |
| Paint chips on hood | Light-colored paint finishes show stone chip damage easily | Touch-up paint recommended for high-speed highway driving |
