## 2007 Harley-Davidson Ultra Classic Electra Glide

## Overview

The 2007 Harley-Davidson Ultra Classic Electra Glide (model code FLHTCU) is the flagship touring motorcycle in Harley-Davidson's Touring family. Built on the Twin Cam 96 engine introduced in 2007, the Ultra Classic is designed for long-distance two-up riding with a full complement of touring equipment: a full fairing with integrated audio and CB radio, hard saddlebags, a Tour-Pak luggage carrier, a comfortable two-up seat, cruise control, and a large front windshield. The 2007 model year was significant as it introduced the larger 96 cubic inch (1,584 cc) Twin Cam engine replacing the previous 88 cubic inch motor.

## Touring Equipment

The FLHTCU Ultra Classic includes the following factory touring equipment:

- **Batwing fairing** — full fairing with integrated instruments and large windshield
- **Saddlebags** — lockable hard Tour-Pak luggage carrier (rear)
- **Hard saddlebags** — lockable, left and right
- **Audio system** — AM/FM/weatherband radio, CD player, integrated fairing speakers, and saddlebag speakers
- **CB radio** — factory-integrated CB with handlebar controls
- **Intercom** — rider-to-passenger intercom
- **Cruise control** — electronic cruise control, handlebar-mounted
- **Air-adjustable rear suspension** — air valve accessible under the seat
- **Footboards** — driver and passenger floorboards
- **Heated grips** — factory heated hand grips
- **Navigation mount** — fairing-integrated GPS mount (GPS unit optional)

## Known Issues and Common Concerns

| Issue | Description | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Twin Cam 96 cam tensioner / cam bearing wear | The 2007 Twin Cam 96 uses hydraulic cam chain tensioners that can wear and allow the cam chain to become noisy or skip. This was a known issue on early Twin Cam engines | Inspect for cam chest noise at startup. Consider upgrading to roller-style cam chain tensioners; many owners upgrade camshafts at the same time |
| Primary chain tensioner | The primary chain shoe (tensioner) can wear and produce a rattle at idle | Replace the primary chain, shoe, and compensating sprocket as a set when noise develops |
| Compensating sprocket | The compensating sprocket (between the engine and primary chain) can wear internally and produce a clunking or banging sound on deceleration | A clunk felt through the left foot when rolling off the throttle is the classic symptom. Replace with the updated Heavy Duty version |
| Fork seal leaks | 41mm fork seals deteriorate over time and will begin weeping oil | Replace seals and wiper seals as a set. Do both legs at the same time |
| Electrical connector corrosion | Multi-pin connectors on the fairing harness and lighting circuits can corrode, causing intermittent electrical faults | Dielectric grease in all connectors reduces issues; inspect during annual maintenance |
| Rear brake caliper drag | Rear brake caliper pins can corrode and cause the rear brake to drag, reducing fuel economy and causing rotor/pad wear | Inspect and lubricate caliper slide pins annually |
| Belt drive wear | Rear drive belt stretches and wears with mileage. A loose belt can jump teeth and cause damage | Inspect belt tension and condition at every oil change |
| Oil consumption | The Twin Cam 96 can consume a small amount of oil, particularly in warm weather | Check oil level every 1,000 miles between changes |
