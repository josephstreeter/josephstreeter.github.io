## John Deere 1025R Compact Utility Tractor with Backhoe

## Overview

The John Deere 1025R is a sub-compact utility tractor (SCUT) in John Deere's 1 Series lineup. Designed for residential and light agricultural use, the 1025R provides versatility through a three-point hitch, a mid-mount PTO for mowers, a front loader, and an optional rear backhoe. Equipped with the 260 Backhoe attachment, the 1025R becomes capable of light excavation, trenching, and landscaping work well beyond its compact footprint.

## Key Features

- **Hydrostatic transmission** — infinitely variable ground speed in two ranges; no clutch pedal required
- **4-wheel drive** — engages automatically or manually depending on model year; provides traction in soft or uneven terrain
- **120R front loader** (if equipped) — quick-attach bucket with single-lever joystick control and float position
- **260 Backhoe** — rear-mount, tool-bar frame mount; removable when not needed
- **Mid-mount mower ready** — 60D mid-mount mower deck attaches under the frame; driven by mid PTO
- **ROPS** — factory-installed two-post rollover protection structure, foldable
- **iTPO rear PTO** — independent rear PTO engages without forward motion
- **Differential lock** — rear diff lock improves traction in slippery conditions; auto-disengage when steering

## Known Issues and Common Concerns

| Issue | Description | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hydraulic fluid overheating | Extended backhoe operation can raise hydraulic temps | Monitor temp; allow rest cycles during prolonged digging; keep hydraulic cooler clean |
| Front axle grease | Front axle pivot and spindles require regular greasing | Neglecting front axle grease leads to rapid wear; grease at every 50 hours |
| Loader float position wear | Loader control valve can wear over heavy use cycles | Use float position correctly when back-dragging |
| R4 tire soil compaction | R4 tires are harder on turf than R3 | Consider R3 turf tires if lawn appearance is a priority |
| Backhoe boom cylinder seal leaks | Hydraulic cylinder seals can weep oil over time | Inspect cylinders at each use; reseal kits available from dealer |
| DEF / Tier 4 emission system | Tier 4 engines require ULSD fuel at all times | Non-ULSD fuel will damage the emissions system and void warranty |
| Cold-weather starting | Compact diesels can be slow to start below 20°F | Allow glow plugs to cycle fully (dashboard wait-to-start light must extinguish) |
