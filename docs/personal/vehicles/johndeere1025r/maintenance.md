## John Deere 1025R Maintenance Guide

## Overview

This page is a maintenance reference for the John Deere 1025R sub-compact utility tractor equipped with the Yanmar 3TNV80F 3-cylinder diesel engine. Service intervals follow the John Deere recommended schedule based on operating hours. The tractor uses an hour meter — all intervals should be tracked by hours, not calendar time, unless stated otherwise.

> [!WARNING]
> Always engage the park brake, lower all attachments to the ground, shut off the engine, and remove the key before performing any maintenance. Install the ROPS lock pin if working under raised implements.

## Fluid Capacities

| Fluid | Capacity | Specification |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine Oil (with filter) | 2.7 quarts | John Deere Plus-50 II 15W-40 or equivalent CK-4 diesel oil |
| Coolant | 3.2 quarts | John Deere Cool-Gard II pre-mix (50/50) |
| Hydraulic / Transmission / Rear Axle (shared sump) | 9.5 quarts | John Deere Hy-Gard (J20C) or Low Viscosity Hy-Gard (J20D) |
| Front Axle Differential | 0.53 quarts (1 pint) | John Deere GL-4 80W gear lube |
| Fuel Tank | 5.3 gallons | Ultra-Low Sulfur Diesel (ULSD, 15 ppm max) |

> [!NOTE]
> The 1025R uses a **combined hydrostatic transmission, hydraulic system, and rear axle** in a single shared fluid sump. Only use John Deere Hy-Gard or Low Viscosity Hy-Gard — other hydraulic fluids are not compatible and will cause damage.

## Service Intervals at a Glance

| Interval | Service Items |
| --- | --- |
| Before each use | Check engine oil level, coolant level, hydraulic fluid level, fuel level, tire pressure, all attachment pins and hardware |
| Every 50 hours | Engine oil and filter, grease all fittings, check battery, check tire pressure, inspect air filter |
| Every 100 hours | Inspect air filter element; replace if needed |
| Every 200 hours | Hydraulic / transmission / rear axle fluid and filter, fuel filter, front axle fluid check |
| Every 400 hours | Engine coolant replacement (or every 2 years) |
| Every 500 hours | Inspect and adjust valve clearance |
| Annually | Inspect all belts, hoses, and hydraulic lines; check ROPS fasteners; test safety interlock switches |

## Pre-Operation Inspection (Before Each Use)

**Steps:**

1. Check the engine oil level on the dipstick on the left side of the engine. Level should be between the ADD and FULL marks. Do not overfill
2. Check the coolant level in the overflow reservoir on the right side of the engine bay. Level should be between MIN and MAX. Do not open the radiator cap when hot
3. Check the hydraulic / transmission fluid level using the sight glass or dipstick on the rear axle housing (right side). Level should be at the full mark with the tractor on level ground and all cylinders retracted (loader down, backhoe stowed)
4. Inspect the fuel level in the tank on the right side of the hood. Fill with ULSD only
5. Walk around the tractor and inspect all attachment pins, Lynch pins, and hardware for security
6. Inspect tires for cuts, punctures, and pressure
7. Verify the ROPS is in the raised and locked position before operating
8. Check that all safety interlock switches function: the tractor should not start in gear or with the PTO engaged

## Every 50 Hours

### Engine Oil and Filter

- **Interval:** Every 50 hours or annually, whichever comes first
- **Oil:** John Deere Plus-50 II 15W-40 (or CK-4 rated diesel oil). Use 0W-40 synthetic in temperatures below 0°F
- **Filter:** John Deere RE504836 (or equivalent)
- **Capacity:** 2.7 quarts with filter

**Required Tools:**

- 17mm wrench or socket (drain plug)
- Oil filter wrench
- Drain pan (minimum 3-quart capacity)
- Torque wrench
- Funnel
- Shop rags
- Nitrile gloves

**Steps:**

1. Run the engine for 5 minutes to warm the oil. Shut the engine off and allow 5 minutes for oil to settle
2. Place a drain pan under the engine oil drain plug on the bottom of the engine block (left-center underside)
3. Remove the 17mm drain plug and allow oil to drain completely
4. Replace the drain plug and torque to **22 ft-lbs**
5. Remove the oil filter (located on the left side of the engine block). Use the oil filter wrench if it is tight
6. Wipe the filter sealing surface clean. Apply a thin film of clean oil to the new filter gasket
7. Thread the new filter on by hand until the gasket contacts the base, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn
8. Add 2.7 quarts of engine oil through the fill cap on the valve cover
9. Start the engine and verify the oil pressure warning light extinguishes within 5 seconds. Check for leaks
10. Shut off the engine, wait 5 minutes, and verify the level on the dipstick

### Grease All Fittings

- **Grease type:** John Deere Multi-Purpose SD Polyurea Grease or equivalent NLGI #2 grease
- **Tool:** Grease gun with standard coupler and needle-nose coupler for hard-to-reach fittings

**Grease Fitting Locations:**

1. **Front axle pivot** (1 fitting) — center of the front axle beam, underneath
2. **Front spindles** (2 fittings, 1 per side) — inner spindle, behind the wheel
3. **Tie rod ends** (2 fittings) — one each end of the tie rod
4. **120R Loader pivot pins** (multiple fittings on boom and bucket pivots) — if equipped
5. **260 Backhoe pivot pins** (multiple fittings on boom, dipper, bucket, and swing pivots) — if equipped; count all pins and confirm each is greased
6. **Rear stabilizer pivot pins** (backhoe stabilizer legs, 2 fittings)
7. **3-point hitch link pivots** (lower links and top link ends)

**Steps:**

1. Wipe each grease zerk clean before attaching the grease gun coupler
2. Pump grease slowly until fresh grease purges from the edge of the seal or joint. Stop when fresh grease appears — over-greasing blows seals
3. Wipe away excess purged grease to prevent dirt accumulation
4. Replace any zerk that will not accept grease (blocked or damaged)

## Every 100 Hours

### Air Filter Inspection and Replacement

- **Interval:** Inspect every 100 hours; replace every 200 hours or when the restriction indicator shows red
- **Primary filter:** John Deere RE504910 (or equivalent)
- **Safety filter (inner):** John Deere RE504911 — replace only when primary is replaced twice, or if damaged

**Required Tools:**

- No tools required for primary filter removal (tool-less clips on most models)
- Compressed air (for cleaning housing only — do not blow air through the filter element)

**Steps:**

1. Locate the air filter housing on the left side of the hood, above the engine
2. Release the housing cover latches and remove the cover
3. Pull the primary (outer) filter element straight out. Do not tap or bang it
4. Inspect the primary filter: if visibly loaded with dust, discolored, or the restriction indicator showed red, replace it. Never wash or compress-air-clean a paper element — this damages it
5. Inspect the inner safety filter element. Replace it only if: it is visibly dirty, there is a hole or tear, or you are replacing the primary for the second time
6. Wipe the inside of the housing with a damp cloth — do not use compressed air inside the housing
7. Install the new primary filter and press firmly until the end cap seats
8. Reinstall the cover and secure the latches
9. Reset the vacuum restriction indicator (if equipped) by pressing the reset button

## Every 200 Hours

### Hydraulic / Transmission / Rear Axle Fluid and Filter

- **Interval:** Every 200 hours or annually
- **Fluid:** John Deere Hy-Gard (J20C) or Low Viscosity Hy-Gard (J20D)
- **Filter:** John Deere MIU801302 suction screen / John Deere hydraulic filter (verify with parts catalog)
- **Capacity:** 9.5 quarts (total system); typical service fill after filter change is approximately 8-9 quarts

> [!WARNING]
> Use only John Deere Hy-Gard or Low Viscosity Hy-Gard fluid. This fluid serves the hydrostatic transmission, hydraulic system, and rear axle simultaneously. Incorrect fluid causes HST failure, hydraulic pump damage, and differential wear.

**Required Tools:**

- Drain pan (minimum 3-gallon capacity)
- 3/4 in or 1 in socket or wrench (drain plug, varies by model year)
- Oil filter wrench (hydraulic filter)
- Torque wrench
- Fluid pump or funnel with long-neck tube
- Shop rags
- Nitrile gloves

**Steps:**

1. Run the tractor for 10 minutes to warm the fluid. Park on level ground, lower all attachments to the ground, and shut the engine off
2. Retract all hydraulic cylinders completely (loader down and curled back, backhoe stowed) — this ensures the system is at minimum volume so you can check the final fill level accurately
3. Place a large drain pan under the rear axle housing drain plug (bottom of the rear axle, underneath the differential)
4. Remove the drain plug. Allow fluid to drain completely (10-15 minutes)
5. Remove the hydraulic filter (located on the right side of the rear axle housing). Have rags ready — residual fluid will spill
6. Apply a thin film of clean Hy-Gard to the new filter gasket. Thread the new filter on by hand and tighten an additional 3/4 turn
7. Reinstall the drain plug and torque to specification (typically **44 ft-lbs** — verify in operator's manual)
8. Locate the hydraulic fluid fill port on top of the rear axle housing (usually a yellow-cap dipstick tube on the right side)
9. Add approximately 8 quarts of Hy-Gard through the fill port using a clean fluid pump or funnel
10. Start the engine and operate all hydraulic functions (loader up/down/curl, backhoe swing/crowd/bucket, 3-point hitch up/down) several times to purge air from the system
11. Shut the engine off. Check the fluid level using the sight glass or dipstick on the rear axle housing. The level should be at the FULL mark with the engine off and all cylinders retracted
12. Add fluid as needed to reach the full mark. Do not overfill
13. Check for leaks at the drain plug and filter

### Fuel Filter

- **Interval:** Every 200 hours or annually
- **Filter:** John Deere RE522878 (spin-on type; verify with parts catalog for your build date)

**Required Tools:**

- Fuel filter wrench or strap wrench
- Drain pan or rags
- Clean diesel to pre-fill the new filter
- Nitrile gloves

**Steps:**

1. Shut the engine off. Locate the fuel filter on the right side of the engine on the fuel supply line
2. Place rags or a small drain pan under the filter to catch spilled fuel
3. Remove the old filter using a strap wrench
4. Pre-fill the new filter with clean diesel fuel up to about 2/3 full to prime the system
5. Apply a thin film of clean diesel to the new filter gasket
6. Thread the new filter on by hand until the gasket seats, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn. Do not over-tighten
7. Turn the ignition key to the ON position (do not crank) for 20-30 seconds to allow the lift pump to prime. Repeat 3-4 times
8. Start the engine — it may crank longer than usual while purging air. This is normal
9. Let the engine idle and inspect the filter for leaks

### Front Axle Differential Fluid Check

- **Interval:** Check every 200 hours
- **Fluid:** John Deere GL-4 80W gear lube

**Steps:**

1. Raise the front of the tractor and support safely
2. Locate the front axle differential — there is a fill/check plug on the axle housing
3. Remove the plug. Fluid should be level with the bottom of the plug hole
4. Add GL-4 80W gear lube if low until it seeps from the plug hole
5. Replace the plug

## Every 400 Hours / Every 2 Years

### Engine Coolant

- **Interval:** Every 400 hours or every 2 years, whichever comes first
- **Type:** John Deere Cool-Gard II pre-mixed (50/50 blend)
- **Capacity:** 3.2 quarts

> [!NOTE]
> Do not use universal green antifreeze or Dex-Cool. John Deere Cool-Gard II is an OAT formulation required for the Yanmar engine.

**Required Tools:**

- Drain pan
- Pliers (lower hose clamp)
- Funnel
- Distilled water (for flush, if needed)
- 3.2 quarts John Deere Cool-Gard II pre-mixed

**Steps:**

1. Allow the engine to cool completely. Never open the cooling system when hot
2. Remove the radiator cap (located under the hood, on the top of the radiator)
3. Locate the lower radiator hose on the driver side and loosen the clamp, or use the petcock if equipped, to drain coolant into a pan
4. Once drained, close the drain and fill with distilled water. Run the engine to temperature with the heater on, drain, and repeat until the water runs clear (flush step — optional but recommended)
5. Refill slowly with Cool-Gard II pre-mixed coolant to avoid air pockets
6. Start the engine with the radiator cap off. Watch for the coolant level to drop as the thermostat opens and air purges. Top off as needed
7. Install the radiator cap once air bubbles stop and the level stabilizes
8. Run to full operating temperature and verify no leaks
9. Allow to cool and recheck the overflow reservoir level

## Every 500 Hours

### Valve Clearance Inspection and Adjustment

- **Interval:** Every 500 hours
- **Intake valve clearance:** 0.006-0.010 in (0.15-0.25 mm) — cold
- **Exhaust valve clearance:** 0.010-0.014 in (0.25-0.35 mm) — cold

**Required Tools:**

- Feeler gauge set (metric and SAE)
- 10mm wrench (valve cover bolts)
- Flathead screwdriver (adjuster screw)
- 10mm wrench (adjuster locknut)
- Top dead center (TDC) reference via flywheel timing marks

> [!NOTE]
> Valve adjustment on a 3-cylinder diesel is straightforward but requires familiarity with finding TDC. If not comfortable with this procedure, have it performed by a John Deere dealer or qualified diesel mechanic.

**Steps:**

1. Allow the engine to cool completely — valve clearance must be set cold
2. Remove the valve cover (typically 4-6 bolts around the perimeter)
3. Rotate the engine to TDC on cylinder 1 compression stroke (both valves closed, timing mark aligned)
4. Check each valve's clearance by sliding the feeler gauge between the rocker arm and valve stem tip. There should be slight drag — not tight, not loose
5. If adjustment is needed: hold the rocker adjuster screw with a flathead screwdriver and loosen the locknut with a 10mm wrench. Turn the adjuster screw until the feeler gauge slides with slight drag. Hold the screw and torque the locknut
6. Rotate the engine 240° for cylinder 2, and another 240° for cylinder 3, adjusting each in sequence
7. Reinstall the valve cover with a new gasket. Torque bolts evenly in a cross pattern to **9 ft-lbs**

## Annual Maintenance

### Drive Belt Inspection

- **Inspect:** All belts for cracking, fraying, glazing, and proper tension
- **Belts to inspect:** Alternator/fan belt, mower deck belt (if mid-mount mower is installed)

**Required Tools:**

- Flashlight
- Straight-edge or belt tension gauge (optional)

**Steps:**

1. With the engine off, locate the accessory belt on the front of the engine
2. Inspect for cracks, missing ribs, glazing, or fraying. Replace if any wear is visible
3. Check tension: deflection at the belt's longest span should be approximately 1/4 to 1/2 in under moderate finger pressure
4. Inspect idler pulley and tensioner bearings by spinning by hand — no grinding or wobble

### Hydraulic Hose and Cylinder Inspection

**Steps:**

1. Inspect all hydraulic hoses (loader, backhoe, 3-point hitch) for: cuts, abrasion, bulging, cracking at the crimp fittings, and oil seeping/sweating
2. Gently wipe down each hydraulic cylinder rod with a clean rag and observe for oil film (normal) vs. active dripping or puddles (seal leak)
3. Inspect all cylinder rod surfaces for pitting, scratches, or rust — damaged rods will rapidly destroy replacement seals
4. Check all hydraulic fittings at the loader, backhoe, and tractor quick-couplers for security

### Safety Interlock Switch Test

**Steps:**

1. Attempt to start the tractor with the PTO engaged — the engine should not crank
2. Attempt to start the tractor with the HST transmission not in neutral — the engine should not crank
3. Raise the operator seat while the engine is running — the engine should shut off within a few seconds
4. Engage the backhoe seat (if equipped with a seat interlock) and verify the tractor cannot move with the backhoe controls engaged and operator off the seat
5. If any interlock fails, do not operate the tractor until repaired

### ROPS Hardware Inspection

**Steps:**

1. Inspect all ROPS mounting bolts at the base of the support frame for corrosion and tightness. Torque to spec if loose (verify in operator's manual — typically 120-140 ft-lbs for M16 grade bolts)
2. Inspect the ROPS pivot hinge (fold-down ROPS models) for wear and pin security
3. Inspect the ROPS lock pin to verify it engages securely in the raised position
4. Do not operate the tractor with the ROPS folded down unless clearance absolutely requires it, and re-raise and lock immediately afterward

## Backhoe-Specific Maintenance (260 Backhoe)

### Grease All Backhoe Pivot Pins

- **Interval:** Every 50 hours (included in standard greasing)
- **Key points:** Boom pivot (frame to boom), boom cylinder pins (top and bottom), dipper pivot (boom to dipper), dipper cylinder pins, bucket pivot pins, swing pivot, stabilizer pivot pins

### Hydraulic Cylinder Seal Inspection

- **Interval:** Inspect before each use; service as needed

**Steps:**

1. Fully extend and retract each backhoe cylinder (boom, dipper, bucket, swing) through its full range of motion
2. Inspect the area around each cylinder wiper seal where the rod exits the barrel for drips or oil film buildup
3. Minor oil film is acceptable. Active dripping or oil accumulation requires cylinder reseal
4. Inspect the cylinder rod surface for surface rust or pitting — clean any surface rust with a fine abrasive cloth and apply light oil

### Bucket Teeth and Cutting Edge

- **Interval:** Inspect every 50 hours; replace when wear is significant

**Steps:**

1. Inspect the backhoe bucket teeth for wear length. Worn-down teeth reduce digging efficiency and can damage the bucket
2. Replace individual teeth using a hammer and drift punch to drive out the retaining pin. Drive the new tooth onto the adapter and secure with a new pin
3. Inspect the cutting edge of the loader bucket (if equipped) for excessive wear or bending. Replace if worn to less than 50% of original thickness
