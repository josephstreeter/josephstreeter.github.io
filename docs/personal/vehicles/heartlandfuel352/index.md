## 2019 Heartland Fuel Toy Hauler Series M-352

## Overview

The 2019 Heartland Fuel Series M-352 is a fifth-wheel toy hauler designed to carry powersports equipment — motorcycles, ATVs, UTVs, and similar gear — in a dedicated rear garage while providing full-featured residential living quarters in the front. Built on Heartland's Fuel platform, the M-352 prioritizes payload capacity, structural rigidity, and dual-use flexibility: the rear garage converts to additional sleeping and lounge space when not hauling equipment. With a GVWR of 18,000 lbs and a cargo carrying capacity of approximately 4,700 lbs, the M-352 is well-suited for towing with a heavy-duty truck such as the RAM 3500. The fifth-wheel design allows for a tighter turning radius and better weight distribution compared to a traditional travel trailer.

The 2014 RAM 3500 is rated for a maximum fifth-wheel towing capacity of 30,000 lbs — well above this trailer's GVWR — but always verify pin weight is within the truck's pin weight rating (typically 6,000-7,500 lbs for the RAM 3500).

## Fifth-Wheel Coupling

- **Kingpin size:** Standard 2-inch SAE kingpin
- **Compatible hitch:** B&W Companion OEM or equivalent 5th wheel hitch rated for 18,000+ lbs GVW and 4,500+ lbs pin weight
- **Minimum truck bed length:** 8 ft recommended for standard hitch placement; 6 ft 4 in with a slider hitch (required for a dually with short bed)

## Known Issues and Common Concerns

| Issue | Description | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Slide-out seal wear | Slide-out rubber seals can dry-crack and fail to seal against rain | Inspect and treat with slide-out seal conditioner each season; replace as needed |
| Landing gear motor strain | Electric landing gear motors can burn out if used to level rather than just raise/drop | Use stabilizer jacks and chocks to level; landing gear is for raising/lowering only |
| Battery drain | House batteries drain quickly when not on shore power due to LP detector, CO detector, and converter draw | Disconnect the negative battery lead or use a battery disconnect when stored |
| Roof A/C gaskets | Roof A/C mounting gaskets can compress over time, allowing rain intrusion | Inspect and reseal each season |
| Ramp door hydraulic cylinder | Hydraulic ramp cylinders can leak oil over time | Inspect before each trip; reseal or replace cylinders as needed |
| Fresh water tank winterization | Insufficient antifreeze can leave water in tank valves and low-points | Use compressed air blow-out method plus antifreeze to all traps |
| Roof seams and vents | Lap sealant around vents, antennas, and seams can crack and allow water infiltration | Inspect entire roof 1-2 times per year and reseal as needed |
| Tire age | ST tires degrade with UV exposure even if tread depth is adequate | Replace trailer tires every 5-7 years regardless of tread; check sidewall date code |
| Onboard fuel station | Fuel station pump can fail if fuel sits for extended periods | Use a fuel stabilizer in the onboard tank; run the pump periodically |
